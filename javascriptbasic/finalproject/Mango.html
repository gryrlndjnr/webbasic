Mango







A mango is a fruit, and when people refer to a "mango fruit," they are typically talking about the edible part of the mango, which is the flesh or pulp that you consume. Mangoes belong to the botanical genus Mangifera, and the edible portion of the fruit is the sweet and juicy flesh that surrounds a large, flat, and inedible seed or pit. This flesh is commonly referred to as "mango meat."


The mango fruit is typically oval or kidney-shaped and can vary in size, color, and taste depending on the specific mango variety. When ripe, the flesh is juicy, sweet, and has a unique and delightful flavor that combines both sweet and tangy notes. It is commonly eaten fresh, either sliced and eaten on its own or incorporated into various dishes, desserts, smoothies, and salsas.


Mangoes are known for their vibrant and often colorful skin, which can be green, yellow, orange, or red when ripe. The skin is typically not edible and needs to be peeled away to access the delicious fruit inside.
